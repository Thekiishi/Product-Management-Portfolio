Overview

The problem with finding affordable housing near work

Urban Co-Living is a web-based platform concept developed as the capstone project for the ProductDive Transition into Product Management programme. The premise came from a real and persistent problem: employees and small business owners in Lagos face a combination of high rent, long commutes, and limited access to verified, affordable housing close to their workplaces.

The proposed solution is a platform that helps users find affordable co-living apartments in good environments near their organisations, with a verified user database to ensure residents are paired with like-minded, trustworthy housemates. The capstone covered the full product management process from research to release planning.

User Research

Starting with real conversations

The research phase began with 15 one-on-one user interviews, each approximately 10 minutes, with a mix of 6 introverted males and 9 extroverted females. The goal was to understand the real pain points behind the housing search, not to validate a solution that had already been decided.

Research Questions

  • What are the major concerns when renting a living space?
  • What are the major reasons for considering a co-living space?
  • What is your current perception of co-living?
  • What are the pain points of co-living for you?
  • How could those pain points be addressed to make co-living viable?

Key Findings

  • High rent was the primary barrier, not preference for private housing
  • Proximity to work was consistently prioritised over space or privacy
  • Concerns about housemate compatibility were the main hesitation around co-living
  • Most users expressed willingness to consider co-living if safety and vetting were addressed
The key insight: Users were not opposed to co-living. They were opposed to co-living without trust. A verified matching system was not a feature — it was the prerequisite that made the product viable at all.
User Personas

Two distinct users, one platform

Fowler
Chiropractor · Age 24 · Lagos · Extrovert
Situation

Recent graduate who just started at a clinic far from home. Exhausted by daily commute and traffic, struggling to find affordable housing in a good environment near her workplace.

Goals

Live close to work. Access public facilities nearby. Find a large, affordable room without the hassle of the full rental market.

Pain Points

Commute fatigue. Cannot find an apartment that fits both her budget and location needs simultaneously.

Uti
Project Manager · Age 27 · Lagos · Introvert
Situation

Works at an EdTech startup. Currently lives with family and wants his own space but cannot afford housing in his office environment independently.

Goals

Find affordable housing near work with sufficient security. Minimise cost while retaining his privacy. Avoid shared working spaces.

Pain Points

Uncomfortable in shared working spaces. Frustrated by the gap between what he can afford and what is available near his office.

User Stories

Defining what each user needs to do

Customer

  • "As a potential customer, I want to be able to sign up easily so that I can view apartments."

Tenant

  • "As a tenant, I want to be able to make complaints on the app so that they can be resolved quickly."

Developer / Internal

  • "As a developer, I want to see the amount of engagement on the app so that I can monitor progress."

Executives

  • Executives identified a summary view of each segment of data as their top priority for v1.
  • Secondary admin workflow scoped out of this release.
Success Metrics

Success metrics were defined before launch planning, tied to specific business outcomes and validated at defined time intervals post-launch.

Reflection

What the capstone clarified

The capstone confirmed something the research phase made clear early: the difference between a product idea and a product is a precise understanding of what the user actually needs, not what you assumed they needed before you started. The verification and matching mechanism was not in the original brief. It came directly from the interviews.

Writing user stories for three distinct user types also highlighted how differently the same product function reads depending on whose perspective you write from. The tenant's complaint feature and the developer's engagement dashboard are technically related but exist for entirely different reasons. Getting that distinction right before spec writing is the kind of clarity that saves a team significant rework later.

This capstone gave me the structured PM vocabulary and process framework to apply to the real delivery work I was already doing. The connection between the two is what this portfolio is built to show.
